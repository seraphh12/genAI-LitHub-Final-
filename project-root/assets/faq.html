What is Generative AI?

Generative AI is an artificial intelligence that can create original content such as text, images, video, audio, or software code in response to a user's prompt or request. It can learn human language, programming languages, art, chemistry, biology, or any complex subject matter and reuse what it knows to solve new problems. It also relies on sophisticated deep learning models and algorithms that simulate the learning and decision-making processes of the human brain.

How does GenAI work?

Generative AI works by exposing neural networks to large datasets, where the model learns to recognize patterns and structures within existing data to generate new content. These models identify and encode the patterns and relationships in huge amounts of data, and then use that information to understand users' natural language requests or questions and respond with relevant new content.

Limitations of Generative AI

1.

Requires oversight

Generative AI models can introduce false or misleading information, and their outputs may contain biased or offensive language learned from training data. Human oversight is critical to prevent flawed outputs from spreading.

2.

Computational Power and Initial Investment

Generative AI models require significant computing power for both training and operation, which can be costly and resource-intensive.

3.

Potential to Converge

Organizations relying on public generative AI tools may produce similar outputs to others because models are often trained on overlapping datasets.

4.

Resistance from Staff and Customers

Staff with established protocols may struggle to adapt, potentially reducing productivity and creating resistance to adoption.

Reference: https://www.oracle.com/apac/artificial-intelligence/generative-ai/what-is-generative-ai/#gai-limitations

Risks of Generative AI

The output of generative AI can be inaccurate

Generative AI can hallucinate and produce false data, made-up quotes, or contradictions, which makes it unreliable as a sole source of truth.

The output of generative AI may not reflect the real world

Models may rely on outdated training data and therefore struggle in environments where up-to-date knowledge is critical.

Generative AI can be used to create realistic fake images, audio, and videos (deepfakes)

AI-generated deepfakes can enable fraud, more convincing phishing, and other types of digital crime.

Use of public generative AI tools may breach data privacy and security requirements

Data input into public tools may be shared or used for future model training, raising privacy and compliance concerns.

Use of generative AI tools may breach copyright and intellectual property requirements

Models trained on copyrighted materials may produce outputs that infringe IP rights; careful review and use of internally owned data can mitigate this risk.
